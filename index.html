How to Check Your SASSA SRD Grant Status: A Step-by-Step Guide

The South African Social Security Agency (SASSA) provides the Social Relief of Distress (SRD) grant to assist individuals who find themselves in financial distress due to various circumstances. The SRD grant has become a lifeline for millions of South Africans, especially in times of economic hardship. If you’ve applied for the SRD grant, it’s essential to stay informed about the status of your application through the SASSA status check, ensuring you are kept up-to-date on whether your application has been approved, rejected, or is pending.

This detailed guide will walk you through the process of checking your SASSA SRD grant status step by step. Whether you applied online, via the SASSA website, or through SMS, this guide will ensure you can easily navigate the system to perform a SASSA status check on your grant application.

Step 1: Visit the Official SASSA SRD Website

The first step in performing a SASSA status check is to visit the official SASSA website. This is the most reliable and secure platform to check your application status.

  • Navigate to the “SRD Grant” section, typically found on the homepage or under the "COVID-19 SRD" tab.

Step 2: Access the SRD Grant Application Portal

Once on the SASSA website, you’ll need to access the SRD Grant Application Portal. This portal allows you to check the status of your application or payment.

  • Look for the "Check Your Status" button or link (usually under the SRD Grant section).
  • Click on the link, and you’ll be redirected to the SRD grant status checking page where you can perform the SASSA status check.

Step 3: Enter Your Personal Information

To check your application status, you’ll need to provide specific details to verify your identity. The information required includes:

  • ID Number or Passport Number: This is the same identification number you provided when applying for the SRD grant.
  • Cellphone Number: This must be the number you used to apply for the SRD grant, as it will be linked to your application.

Double-check that you have the correct details as per your original application. Ensure that the number you input matches exactly, including the country code (+27 for South African numbers).

Step 4: Submit the Information

Once you've entered the required details, click on the “Submit” button. The system will process your request and check your application status. This may take a few moments.

Step 5: View Your SRD Grant Status

After processing your request, the website will display your SRD grant status. Depending on the outcome of your application, you might see one of the following messages:

Common Status Messages:

  • Approved: Your application has been approved, and you are eligible for the SRD grant. You will be informed about the payment schedule and method.
  • Rejected: Your application has been rejected. In this case, the system may provide reasons for the rejection (e.g., you earn an income that exceeds the required threshold).
  • Pending: Your application is still being processed. This means SASSA is reviewing your application, and you should check back later.
  • Under Review: This status indicates that additional information is being processed or verified.
  • Payment Made: This message indicates that a payment has been successfully processed to your selected payment method.

Step 6: Request Reconsideration (If Applicable)

If your SRD grant application is rejected, SASSA allows you to request reconsideration. The process for reconsideration is as follows:

  • Review the Reason for Rejection: Understand why your application was rejected. Common reasons include exceeding the income threshold, being employed, or having other forms of government support.
  • Submit a Request for Reconsideration: You can submit a request for reconsideration through the SASSA website or by contacting SASSA’s call center. Ensure that all the required documents and evidence supporting your case are submitted.
  • Wait for Response: SASSA will review your reconsideration request. It’s essential to check your status regularly to see if your reconsideration was successful.

Step 7: Use the SASSA WhatsApp Service for Updates

For more convenient and real-time updates, SASSA offers a WhatsApp service. You can send a message to the official SASSA WhatsApp number: 082 046 8553. This service allows you to check your application status, as well as receive updates on payments and other grant-related inquiries.

  • Steps to Use WhatsApp Service:
  • Add the number 082 046 8553 to your contacts.
  • Send a message saying “Status” to initiate the status check.
  • Follow the prompts and provide the necessary information (ID number and cellphone number).
  • Wait for the response, which will give you your current SRD grant status.

Step 8: Checking via SMS (Alternative Method)

If you don’t have access to the internet, you can check your SRD grant status via SMS. This method works for both new applications and existing applicants.

How to Check Your Status via SMS:

  • Send an SMS: Text the word “STATUS” followed by your ID number to #134*7737. (For example: STATUS 1234567890123)
  • Receive a Response: Within a few minutes, you will receive an SMS that provides your application status.

Step 9: Keep Your Information Updated

It’s crucial to ensure that your personal details, such as your contact number, banking information, and income status, are kept updated on the SASSA database. Any changes to your circumstances may impact your eligibility or payment.

  • You can update your details via the SASSA SRD website or by calling SASSA's call center for assistance.
  • Make sure your payment method (bank account, Post Office, or other) is correctly linked to your profile to avoid delays in receiving funds.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

While checking your SRD grant status is generally a straightforward process, you may encounter some issues. Here are a few common problems and how to resolve them:

1. Incorrect Details

If you enter incorrect information (e.g., ID number or cellphone number), the system will not provide your status. Ensure all details are accurate and double-check your records.

2. System Unavailability

Sometimes, the SASSA website or SMS services may be temporarily unavailable due to maintenance or technical issues. Try again after a few hours or use an alternative method (WhatsApp or SMS).

3. Long Processing Times

If your application status is showing as “Pending” or “Under Review” for an extended period, it may be due to backlogs or increased applications. Be patient and continue checking regularly.

Conclusion

By following this step-by-step guide, you can easily track your SRD grant status and stay on top of your application. Regularly checking your SASSA status check will help you stay informed about your financial assistance.
